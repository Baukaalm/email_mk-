We focus on finding and integrating best waste management practices and energy-saving technologies in construction. We help to structure and streamline waste management process to optimise cost expenditure and improve the environmental situation in Kazakhstan.
We've been operating for three years in Kazakhstan, and today we would like to tell you what we have accomplished this summer. This a story about the project we are proud to share with you. It's about a collection, recycling, temporary storage and hauling of solid waste during Astana EXPO-2017.
Background
International Specialised Exhibition EXPO-2017 was held in Astana this summer. It is the principal event for Kazakhstan with its international image and future economic development at stake.
It was the first time, so many international and regional guests came to visit Astana. During offpeak days around 50,000 people attended the exhibition, reaching up to 110,000 people during weekends and holidays. To compare, Barys-Arena Ice Stadium accommodates 11,578 people, meaning that we serviced ten fully occupied stadiums per day!
Event planners had to go full throttle to prepare the city and its infrastructure for the arrival of our guests. Many systems had to be tested and further improved as they were not initially designed for the predicted number of visitors, including transportation, accommodation, services and waste management. If only one of these issues was overlooked, Kazakhstan could have potentially lost its face.
What would have happened
1
EXPO utility services would have been confronted with an enormous task of dealing with the same amount of waste as the whole Astana city produces during a similar period.
2
Standard municipal rubbish bins would have been full in the first 60 minutes of the exhibition opening. As a general rule at such events, waste cannot be dealt with by regular means during open hours, thus creating localised garbage dump near every waste bin.
3
As the exhibition is a summer event, the smell would have been relentless. If we take into account Astana’s strong winds as well, the waste mismanagement would have quickly spelt disaster.

We have developed and established Kazakhstan’s first separate waste collection and recycling process. The system held up to advanced local requirements and highest international standards.
First, we evaluated and calculated the amount and types of waste to be produced, then selected the most efficient equipment that would accommodate all requirements. Each equipment from bin to container was carefully allocated its place, which would maximise service efficiency.
Bigbelly Solar smart bins were installed to collect waste and recyclables at the exhibition area. They are solar-powered containers with integrated mechanical presses for waste compaction. Every smart bin is separated into three segments, which allows separate waste collection. The non-reclaimable waste section has a compaction press inside to reduce the waste volume to 10 times. It allowed us to save on costs of bins. Otherwise, we would have needed around 30 times more conventional bins.

Separate waste collection facilitates a higher volume of waste recycling: each bin is divided into three sections (paper/cardboard, plastic and mixed waste). The main thing is that this approach makes people get into the habit to separate their own waste (up to 90% of bin content was separated correctly at least in the last exhibition days). We hope it would now be more natural for Kazakh people to adopt this separate waste collection culture to their daily life.

Imagine: conventional steel waste container we may often see in the backyards can accommodate only 0.75 cubic meters. If we put all waste in these containers, we would require 23,412 of them. Since we accumulated waste in mobile presscontainers, we only needed 13.
We provided services not only to Kazakhstan part of the exhibition but also the entire area (25Ha), i.e. 21 international organisations and over 100 visiting countries. Over 3,000 events (including those of event planners) from business meetings and world star concerts were held.
During the active phase of the exhibition over 42% of all waste was treated. It was not our mandatory objective, but we still did our best. We needed to do decide the best way to deal with separated waste, and we agreed on recycling.
 
We are very proud that we managed to remove all waste and recycling during the only possible time: from 01:00 to 07:00am, thanks to efficient planning. Visitors never saw any waste hauling vehicles at all.
We also disposed of all construction waste upon pavilion deconstruction phase, estimating around 14,170 cubic meters in total with 87% sent to landfill and remaining 13% processed and recycled. It is not a bad start as construction waste recycling is almost never done in Kazakhstan. All construction and property development companies will be required to recover their construction waste starting from January 2018. However, there is no such requirement currently.
